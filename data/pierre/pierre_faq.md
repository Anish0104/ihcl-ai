# The Pierre — Frequently Asked Questions

## Q1. What time is check-in at The Pierre?
A. Standard check-in begins at 3:00pm. Guests may request early arrival support, but early check-in is based on availability and may involve additional fees.

## Q2. What time is check-out?
A. Standard check-out is 12:00pm noon. Late check-out can sometimes be arranged, but it is subject to availability and potential charges.

## Q3. Can the hotel guarantee early check-in or late check-out?
A. No. Both early arrival access and late departure are requests rather than guarantees and depend on occupancy and operational conditions.

## Q4. What is the minimum age to check in?
A. Guests must be at least 18 years old and present valid government-issued identification at check-in.

## Q5. What is the occupancy policy, and do children stay free?
A. The maximum standard occupancy is 2 adults per room. Children 17 and under stay free when using existing bedding in the room of a paying adult.

## Q6. Are extra beds available?
A. Yes, but only in select rooms. The charge is $50 plus tax per night, and the hotel recommends requesting it in advance.

## Q7. What kinds of accommodations does The Pierre offer?
A. The Pierre offers 189 rooms and 49 suites. Categories vary, and guests may have city, Upper East Side, Fifth Avenue, or Central Park-oriented outlooks depending on assignment. Exact room type and live availability must be confirmed by the hotel.

## Q8. What is the pet policy?
A. The hotel welcomes dogs only, with a maximum of 2 dogs per room and a maximum weight of 15 pounds per dog. The fee is $75 per day per dog.

## Q9. What pet restrictions and amenities should guests know about?
A. Dogs must be leashed except in the room, may not be left unattended, and are not allowed in restaurants or the fitness center. A dog waiver is required at check-in. On request, the hotel can provide a dog bed, bowls, and homemade treats, and concierge may help arrange pet sitting. Certified Service Animals are always welcome at no charge.

## Q10. What is the daily facility fee?
A. The facility fee is $30 per day, or $34.43 with tax.

## Q11. What is included in the facility fee?
A. The fee includes high-speed WiFi for up to 5 devices, morning coffee and tea in the lobby, PressReader with unlimited digital newspapers and magazines, unlimited local and domestic calls, and Bedside Reading digital content.

## Q12. What is the payment and pre-authorization policy?
A. A credit card pre-authorization is required at arrival for the value of the stay plus $200 per day in incidentals, capped at $600. Cash-paying guests must prepay the stay in full and provide $150 per day for incidentals.

## Q13. Does The Pierre have smoking and non-smoking rooms?
A. Yes. Both smoking and non-smoking rooms are available, and guests should specify their preference at reservation whenever possible.

## Q14. What happens with early departure?
A. Early departure results in a fee equal to one night's room and tax.

## Q15. What dining venues are available at the hotel?
A. The main dining options are Perrine, Two E Bar and Lounge, and The Rotunda. The hotel also offers room service, private dining, and limited-engagement culinary experiences.

## Q16. What is Perrine, and how do guests reserve it?
A. Perrine is The Pierre's modern American fine dining restaurant led by Executive Chef Vincenzo Garofalo. During warmer periods, guests may dine on the Rose Terrace with Central Park views. Reservations can be made through Resy via perrinenyc.com or through concierge.

## Q17. What are room service hours?
A. Room service breakfast runs from 6:45am to 11:00am. Lunch and dinner room service is available from 11:00am to 10:45pm.

## Q18. What should guests know about Two E Bar and Lounge?
A. Two E is the hotel's cocktail and lounge venue, originally the Gentleman's Library and later redesigned by Alexandra Champalimaud. It offers bespoke cocktails, signature mocktails, curated wines, and seasonal lite bites. Food preparation typically takes 20 to 25 minutes.

## Q19. When is live jazz at Two E?
A. Live jazz takes place every Thursday, Friday, and Saturday from 8:00pm to 11:00pm. No reservations are required.

## Q20. Does The Pierre serve afternoon tea?
A. Afternoon tea may be offered seasonally or through special programming. Because schedules can change, guests should confirm current service directly with the hotel or concierge.

## Q21. What is The Rotunda?
A. The Rotunda is one of The Pierre's signature spaces, known for Edward Melcarth's 1967 Neoclassical trompe l'oeil murals. It is used for light bites, seasonal events, and private functions.

## Q22. Is private dining available?
A. Yes. The hotel offers private dining for corporate dinners, celebrations, and small weddings. Inquiries can be directed to 212-838-8000.

## Q23. What is the Magic Table Experience?
A. The Magic Table Experience is a limited-engagement immersive dining concept by Chef Vincenzo, combining interactive magic, 3D storytelling, and food. Reservations are handled through themagictable.com.

## Q24. How much event space does The Pierre offer?
A. The hotel has approximately 18,000 square feet of event space, supporting weddings, meetings, gala dinners, and hybrid functions.

## Q25. What is the capacity of the Grand Ballroom?
A. The Grand Ballroom can host up to 500 guests for a reception or 350 for a seated dinner.

## Q26. What is the capacity of the Cotillion Room?
A. The Cotillion Room can host up to 300 guests for a reception or 200 for a seated dinner. It features 18-foot ceilings, three crystal chandeliers, and a private entrance from 61st Street.

## Q27. What are the capacities of The Rotunda and the meeting rooms?
A. The Rotunda can host up to 80 guests for a reception or 50 for a seated event. The hotel also has 6 meeting rooms for groups of about 14 to 75 guests.

## Q28. Can the hotel handle weddings, corporate events, and hybrid conferences?
A. Yes. Weddings are supported through thepierrecentralparkweddings.com, meetings through thepierremeetings.com, and large conference setups can scale from 250 to 1,000 guests. Hybrid capability through King Cole AV can support more than 10,000 virtual guests.

## Q29. Is there a fitness center?
A. Yes. The Pierre has a fitness center available for guests.

## Q30. Does the hotel offer spa services?
A. Wellness support is available through in-room spa services arranged via an on-call therapist. Guests should request assistance through the hotel team or concierge.

## Q31. Is parking available?
A. Yes. Valet parking is available. Current rates and handling details should be confirmed directly with the hotel.

## Q32. How far is The Pierre from the airports?
A. Typical car estimates are JFK about 45 to 60 minutes and roughly $70 to $80, LaGuardia about 20 to 40 minutes and roughly $40 to $55, and Newark about 45 to 70 minutes and roughly $75 to $95. Actual travel time depends on traffic.

## Q33. What subway access is closest to the hotel?
A. The closest option is the N, R, W, and Q trains at 59th Street and 5th Avenue, about a 4-minute walk south. Other nearby options include the 4, 5, and 6 at 59th and Lexington and the F and Q at 63rd and Lexington.

## Q34. Is WiFi included?
A. Yes. High-speed WiFi is included through the facility fee and supports streaming-quality use on up to 5 devices.

## Q35. Does The Pierre participate in a loyalty program?
A. Yes. The Pierre is part of IHCL and aligns with the Taj InnerCircle loyalty ecosystem.

## Q36. Is The Pierre part of American Express Fine Hotels and Resorts?
A. The hotel is associated with premium luxury travel channels, including American Express Fine Hotels and Resorts. Current member benefits should be confirmed live at booking.

## Q37. What is the relationship between The Pierre, IHCL, and Tata Group?
A. The Pierre is operated by IHCL, Indian Hotels Company Limited, which is part of the Tata Group. IHCL acquired it in 2005 as its North American flagship.

## Q38. Is The Pierre a landmark, and what ratings does it hold?
A. Yes. The Pierre has been a New York City Landmark since 1981. It also holds AAA Five Diamond status and Forbes Five-Star recognition, and it is the only Upper East Side hotel with the AAA Five Diamond distinction.

## Q39. What does Les Clefs d'Or mean at The Pierre?
A. It means the concierge team is connected to the international network of elite professional concierges, reflecting a very high standard of service, discretion, and destination access.

## Q40. What can the concierge arrange for guests?
A. Concierge can assist with restaurant reservations, theatre tickets, car service, personal shopping, tour coordination, medical referrals, and certain specialty support such as pet sitting. For live reservation records, guest history, or current room inventory, the production system connected to PMS and CRM would be needed.
