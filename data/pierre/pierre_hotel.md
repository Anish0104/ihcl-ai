# The Pierre, A Taj Hotel — Hotel Overview

The Pierre, A Taj Hotel is IHCL's North American flagship luxury hotel, positioned at 2 East 61st Street at Fifth Avenue, New York, NY 10065. The main hotel phone number is 212-838-8000. Reservations can be reached at 212-940-8101, and the hotel email is thepierre.ny@tajhotels.com.

The hotel opened in 1930 and was founded by Charles Pierre Casalasco. In 1938 it was acquired by Getty Oil magnate John Paul Getty, further cementing its place among New York's most storied addresses. From 1981 to 2005 it was managed by Four Seasons. In 2005, IHCL, part of the Tata Group, acquired The Pierre as its North American flagship. Ratan Tata personally identified the property as strategically important, aligning it with IHCL's long-term global luxury ambitions.

The Pierre completed a 100 million dollar renovation in 2009, refreshing the guest experience while preserving the historic character that defines the hotel. It now offers 189 rooms and 49 suites. The hotel is known for a staff-to-guest ratio of 3:1, reflecting the service intensity expected of a global luxury flagship.

The Pierre holds a AAA Five Diamond rating and is the only property on the Upper East Side with that distinction. It is also Forbes Five-Star rated. The concierge team includes Les Clefs d'Or professionals, reinforcing the hotel's reputation for polished, deeply connected service.

Architecturally, The Pierre is one of the defining buildings on the Manhattan skyline. The property stands 525 feet tall and has been a designated New York City Landmark since 1981. Its location at Fifth Avenue and 61st Street places it directly on the edge of Central Park and at the gateway to the Upper East Side.

The Pierre has long been associated with notable cultural and social history:

- Coco Chanel stayed at The Pierre in 1932.
- Audrey Hepburn celebrated her Academy Award here in 1954.
- Elizabeth Taylor married Eddie Fisher and bought an apartment in the building in 1959.
- The famous tango scene from *Scent of a Woman* starring Al Pacino was filmed in the Cotillion Room in 1995.
- Anne Hathaway's character dressed for the Met Gala at The Pierre in *Ocean's 8* in 2018.
- Karl Lagerfeld once said he discovered New York from The Pierre.
- Shakira prepared for a Vogue photoshoot at the hotel in 2024.

The Pierre celebrated its 95th anniversary in 2025, underscoring its enduring place in New York hospitality. Within IHCL's Accelerate 2030 strategy, the property exemplifies the Taj brand's presence in global gateway cities: iconic, high-touch, culturally significant hotels that strengthen IHCL's international luxury portfolio.

For staff, the most important positioning message is this: The Pierre is not simply a luxury hotel. It is a landmark Fifth Avenue institution with an unusually deep blend of heritage, location, prestige, and personalized service. Guests may be leisure travelers, dignitaries, long-stay residents, special occasion visitors, fashion and arts clientele, or corporate travelers who expect discretion and flawless execution.
